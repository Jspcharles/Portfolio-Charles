About

Working at the edge of climate risk and machine learning

I'm a PhD candidate researching how explainable AI can reduce basis risk — the gap between an insurance payout and a farmer's actual loss — in weather index insurance for agribusiness. My thesis, Deep Learning the Drought: Explainable AI Approaches for Reducing Basis Risk in Weather Index Insurance for Agribusiness, sits at the intersection of deep learning, drought index modelling (SPI/SPEI), and interpretability methods like SHAP and attention weights.

Most of my work is grounded in two real places: the Queensland and Western Australian wheatbelts, where I model drought-conditioned wheat yield at the pixel and cell level, and try to explain — not just predict — why a model expects a loss.

Research

A six-paper thesis, built around one question

How much of an insurance payout gap can we close by making the model explain itself?

Queensland wheatbelt

PDNet — Two-stage drought yield network

A two-stage architecture: Potential-YieldNet estimates attainable yield, then a dual-branch Yield-Net predicts realised yield under drought using separate climate and SPEI-3/SPEI-6 branches. SHAP analysis is used to frame model behaviour as an insurance basis-risk finding, not just a prediction score.

Status: In preparation

WA wheatbelt · cross-regional validation

Residual-Attention LSTM for yield anomalies

Pixel-level detrended yield anomaly prediction across thousands of grid cells, using attention and SHAP for interpretability, plus resilience-zone clustering (low/medium/high) built from simulated drought severity.

Status: In preparation

Methods & literature

Systematic review — basis risk & index insurance

A structured review of how basis risk is measured and mitigated in weather index insurance, forming the methodological backbone for the rest of the thesis.

Status: Under review

Applied hydrology

Gap-aware imputation for daily reservoir data

A side project applying the same interpretability-first mindset to a 35-year daily observational record, routing different types of data gaps to the imputation method best suited to each.

Status: Manuscript in revision
